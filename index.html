About

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Computer Science at Kansas State University. I earned my Ph.D. and M.Tech (Research) in Computer Science from the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore. My research focuses on formal methods, program verification, and the safety of AI-enabled cyber-physical systems. I also have extensive teaching experience in programming, algorithms, and automata theory.

Research Vision

My research aims to establish a formal foundation for trustworthy autonomy — ensuring that AI-based systems operate safely, reliably, and transparently. I develop methods that combine symbolic reasoning with data-driven learning to verify and certify AI-controlled cyber-physical systems such as autonomous vehicles and intelligent power grids.

My current work focuses on designing scalable verification algorithms, bridging learning and logic through abstraction, and formulating formal contracts and conformance relations for neural controllers. The ultimate goal is a verification ecosystem that allows engineers to design AI systems by proof — integrating safety and correctness throughout their lifecycle.
