About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Computer Science at Kansas State University, working with Prof. Pavithra Prabhakar.

I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore, under the supervision of Dr. Deepak D’Souza. My research lies at the intersection of Formal Methods, Program Verification, and AI Safety.

I develop frameworks that combine logical reasoning and data-driven models to verify systems that integrate perception and control. I am particularly interested in connecting rigorous verification with machine learning to ensure correctness, safety, and interpretability of intelligent autonomous systems.

Research Vision & Interests

My research aims to establish a foundation for trustworthy autonomy by developing verification techniques that make AI-based systems reliable and provably safe. I focus on combining symbolic reasoning with data-driven learning to verify and certify cyber-physical systems such as autonomous vehicles and intelligent power grids.

Projects

Camera-Based Autonomous Systems

Verifying safety in 3D environments using interval image abstraction and falsification methods to identify unsafe trajectories.

Power-Grid Networks with IEDs

Formal reasoning of power grid networks integrated with Intelligent Electronic Devices (IEDs) to enhance reliability and security.

Conformance Checking for DNN

Examining conformance between neural networks to quantify deviations in closed-loop system behaviors across different controllers.

Separation Kernel Verification

Developed "conditional parametric refinement" to formally verify the correctness of the Muen separation kernel generator.
