About

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Computer Science at Kansas State University. I earned my Ph.D. & M.Tech (Research) in Computer Science from the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore. My research lies at the intersection of formal methods, program verification, and AI/cyber-physical system safety. I have strong teaching experience in programming, algorithms, and automata theory.

Research Vision

My long-term research vision is to build a formal foundation for trustworthy autonomy — a world where AI-enabled systems can be deployed with mathematical guarantees of safety, reliability, and accountability.

I develop methods that unify symbolic reasoning with data-driven learning to verify and certify AI-controlled cyber-physical systems such as autonomous vehicles and smart grids. My work focuses on designing scalable verification algorithms, bridging learning and logic through abstraction, and formulating formal contracts and conformance relations for neural controllers. Ultimately, I envision a verification ecosystem that enables engineers to design AI systems by proof — embedding safety and correctness throughout their lifecycle.
